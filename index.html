Lori Roussey

My name's Lori. I am a lawyer specialised in European Data Protection and Cybersecurity Law and Data Rights. My main areas of activities are humanitarian aid, technological regulation and digital rights litigation.
I have just finished a research project to find out if the increasing reliance of administrations on external service providers (public private partnerships) to deal with national security tasks negatively impacts individuals in their right to good and/or accountable administration, as well as freedom to access information. One of my most interesting finding based on the data analysed was that notorious NGOs get much better “transparency” than unknown citizens asking for information they have a right to know. More to come soon.
In the past years I have done research on specific topics tied to the healthy digitalisation of our societies or that bear critical regulatory stakes.
